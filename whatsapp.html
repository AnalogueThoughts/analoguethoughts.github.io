Whatsapp vs Signal

Telegram

In response to Whatsapp's privacy policy update, lots of users have recently migrated to Telegram, seen by many as the secure alternative to Whatsapp. While Telegram might boast some nice features, this is something that I can only understand as the result of a wonderful marketing team. These are some of the reasons I struggle to understand the switch to Telegram (even from Whatsapp!!).
  • Telegram chats are not encrypted per default, which in practice means most conversations will not be encrypted (note, Whatsapp does encrypt all conversations). One could say, well, but you can always use the alternative encrypted mode. And indeed, you could, but that creates an unnecessary differentiation between users. "If you have nothing to hide, why would you choose to hide your communications", it singles out the users who choose to activaye it. See, for example, the case of using Tor, a browser that anonymizes your traffic. Privacy, encryption in this case, should be by default!
  • Unencrypted Telegram messages are stored on Telegram's servers.
  • Telegram group chats are not encrypted
